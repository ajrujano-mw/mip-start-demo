The diet problem

May 29, 2019 · 2 min read
Aster Santana
CEO and founder of MipWise

The Diet Problem, also known as Stigler diet, is a classic optimization problem widely used to illustrate a use case of linear programming. You can find much more details on Wikipedia: Stigler Diet

Here is a short statement of the problem:

Given a set of foods and a set of nutrients, the goal is to make a selection of food and the respective quantity to compose the least expensive diet that meets min and max recommended intake of nutrients.

Input schema​

We can then organize the input data for the Diet Problem in three tables, foods, nutrients, and foods_nutrients, where the first two are master tables.
